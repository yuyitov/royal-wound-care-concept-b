Overview

What is Wound VAC Therapy?

Wound VAC (Vacuum-Assisted Closure) therapy — also called Negative Pressure Wound Therapy (NPWT) — is a medical treatment that uses controlled negative pressure to promote wound healing. A sealed dressing connected to a small pump removes fluid from the wound, helping reduce swelling, improve blood flow, and support the formation of new tissue.

It is commonly used for chronic wounds, surgical wounds, pressure injuries, diabetic foot ulcers, and traumatic wounds that require advanced care beyond standard dressings. Wound VAC therapy is typically ordered and managed by a physician and requires ongoing clinical oversight to ensure proper application and response.

Royal Wound Care can help patients, families, and facility care teams understand whether Wound VAC therapy may be appropriate, review next steps, and coordinate supply and equipment access for eligible cases.

When Wound VAC may be considered

  • Chronic or non-healing wounds Wounds that have not responded adequately to standard wound care over time.
  • Surgical wound dehiscence Post-surgical wounds that have reopened and require advanced healing support.
  • Diabetic foot ulcers Complex foot wounds associated with diabetes-related circulation and healing concerns.
  • Pressure injuries (Stage III & IV) Deep pressure wounds requiring structured drainage and tissue support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Wound VAC Support — Common Questions

What is the supply-based pump access model?

Royal Wound Care's supply-based model means that for eligible cases, the Wound VAC pump may be provided at no separate rental fee when the patient or facility purchases the required dressings, canisters, and accessories through Royal Wound Care.

This model is subject to clinical eligibility review, physician approval, and ongoing supply purchase. Not every case qualifies, and the care team reviews each situation individually before coordination begins.

Does my wound qualify for Wound VAC therapy?

Wound VAC therapy is appropriate for specific wound types and clinical situations. Common candidates include chronic non-healing wounds, deep pressure injuries (Stage III and IV), diabetic foot ulcers, post-surgical wound dehiscence, and traumatic wounds requiring advanced care.

A physician must review and confirm clinical appropriateness before Wound VAC therapy begins. Submit an interest inquiry and the Royal Wound Care team will follow up to begin the review process.

What supplies are needed for Wound VAC therapy?

Wound VAC therapy requires specialized dressings (foam or gauze), a canister to collect wound drainage, a sealed adhesive drape, and connecting tubing. The pump unit itself is also required. The exact supplies depend on the wound type, care setting, and the clinical team's protocol.

Royal Wound Care can help coordinate supply ordering and walk patients or facilities through what is needed for their specific situation.

Can Wound VAC therapy be provided at home or in a skilled nursing facility?

Yes. Wound VAC therapy can be managed in multiple care settings, including the home, skilled nursing facilities, and outpatient wound care clinics. The appropriate setting depends on the patient's overall condition, wound status, and care team availability.

Royal Wound Care supports patients across settings in the Los Angeles area and works with home health teams and skilled nursing facilities to coordinate supply and clinical support.
